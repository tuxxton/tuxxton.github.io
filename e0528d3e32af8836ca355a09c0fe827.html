Are Relationships Harder In 2020?

Are Relationships Harder In 2020?

In 2018, when I first heard the Tank and The Bangas’ song, Rollercoasters, it seemed like the perfect metaphor for many of the relationships I’d both witnessed and participated in. Love was always been akin to the butterflies of an unsettled stomach. Often leaving us wondering if it was worth it at all. I, myself have never been a fan of roller coasters. Though I’ve ridden my fair share, they always seem like a friend to death. If you’d asked me back in January 2020 for a better metaphor, I’d have been hard-pressed to find one. It’s funny how this year has at every turn, also seemed to be a friend to death — a roller coaster, perhaps, a more intricately constructed one. I — we — didn’t have the words for it. Yet, in one fell swoop came Jada Pinkett-Smith and August Alsina, a rise in COVID-19 cases, continued global protests of anti-blackness, more deaths of Black people at the hands of law enforcement, and well, July 2020. With them, they brought an accurate descriptor we’d had yet to consider, entanglement. Could there be a more fitting name for this year, if we tried? A complicated or compromising situation — 2020, thy name is entanglement but what does/will that really mean? What’s done in the dark eventually comes to the light

This year has been one of illumination thus far. It has put a spotlight on and/or dragged to the forefront so many unexpected things. Chief amongst them is that our perceptions are often far from reality. Reality is not ever as neat or digestible as we’d like. Relationships that exist in our reality aren’t ever as simple or clear cut as we pretend that they are and this year is pulling our perceptions of everything apart. From Black people’s relationships to the global power structure (especially in America) to society’s relationship with our healthcare systems and leaders to our interpersonal and romantic relationships, there have been so many twists, turns, and tumultuous moments. What will all of this upheaval really mean for our relationships and our ability to navigate them when we begin to untangle this entanglement we’ve woven so well? Are relationships as we’ve always known them possible or will this “new normal” force us to reexamine our approach to them?
